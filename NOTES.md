Working doc. Not published to the site. Ideas to flesh out, things to consider.

## Priority

### ⚑ Kids Asking About the Divorce (and Related Hard Questions)
High value, needs its own doc. Kids will probe both parents separately, and
consistency matters. Scope includes:
- The divorce itself: age-appropriate answers that are honest without over-sharing
- "Why doesn't [partner] sleep over?" — rooted in co-parenting trust dynamics,
  not a simple logistics question. Need a scripted, calm answer that doesn't
  implicate the co-parent or require justifying the relationship.
- Other open-ended questions about the household structure, living arrangements,
  why things are different from before

This probably pairs closely with the Co-Parenting doc (BND-REL-001) and may
cross-reference Conflict Isolation for situations where questions come up at
difficult moments.

## Possible Future Docs

### Co-Parenting Communication Channel Protocol
References to "P3/P4 channels (Email/App)" exist in Conflict Isolation and the
SLA but there's no actual spec of what goes where. To flesh out:
- What channel is used for what type of communication (text = time-sensitive
  logistics, email = anything that needs a record, app = default co-parenting
  channel)
- Response time expectations per channel
- What is never appropriate in a real-time channel (financial disputes, emotional
  topics, anything that needs a paper trail)

### Holiday & Special Occasion Protocol
Already established in the divorce decree — refer to that document as the
authoritative source. A future note here could summarize the key terms for
quick reference, but the decree controls. Do not create a parallel policy that
could conflict with legal standing.

### Monthly Relationship Check-In
A structured rhythm between partner and I — not a grievance session, more of a
scheduled temperature check. Might not be a "boundary" per se, more of a shared
protocol or agreement. Questions to figure out:
- Does this belong here, or is it a separate shared doc/agreement?
- What does the agenda look like? Open floor vs. structured prompts?
- Frequency confirmed: monthly.

### Household Cleanliness Standard
Covers the day-to-day stuff — dishes, counter/sink, laundry, trash, clean-as-you-go.
Topics to flesh out:
- Dishes: washed or in dishwasher promptly, not left in sink
- Side of the sink: kept clear
- Laundry: not left in machines or piled up
- Trash: taken out, not left sitting around
- General: clean-as-you-go as the default behavior, not a cleanup event

Open question: is this a boundary doc (with enforcement), a house rule posted
somewhere physical, or both? Applies to everyone in the household so probably
fits here alongside Basic Dignity Standard.
