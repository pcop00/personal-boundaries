# Boundary: Physical Integrity & Interactive Play (Kids)

## 1. The Line

**Physical contact is strictly categorized into "Play" (consensual/joyful) or "Reaction" (frustrated/aggressive); I do not accept "Reaction" contact, regardless of my ability to "handle" the pain.**

## 2. Rationale

Allowing kids to hit out of frustration because "I'm tough" teaches them that physical aggression is an acceptable outlet for anger. To protect their future relationships and my own physical autonomy, I must enforce a "Zero-Grievance Hitting" policy while maintaining the play that builds our bond.

## 3. Implementation

- **The "Hands-Off" Transition:** If a game of wrestling or rough-housing shifts from laughter to frustration, the game is **immediately** terminated.
- **The "Toughness" Decoupling:** I will stop using my physical strength as a reason to allow hitting. The rule is based on the *intent* of the touch, not the *impact* of the blow.
- **The Interception:** When a frustrated hit is attempted, I will physically block/intercept the hand (without counter-aggression) and deploy the [SIGNAL].

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - The Immediate Halt]:** "Hands off. I don't play when we're angry. We are done with this game until we are both calm."
- **[SIGNAL - The Logic Check]:** "I'm tough enough to handle that hit, but I'm too important to be hit out of anger. If you're frustrated, use your words or walk away."
- **[SIGNAL - The Re-Entry]:** "I'm ready to play again when you can keep your hands in 'fun mode' instead of 'mad mode.' Are you ready yet?"

!!! warning "Enforcement Step"
    If a hit occurs out of frustration, I will immediately create 5–10 feet of physical distance. I will not lecture while in range of another hit; I will wait until the child's nervous system has cooled down before discussing the boundary.
