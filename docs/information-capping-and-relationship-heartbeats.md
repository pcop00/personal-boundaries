# Boundary: Information Capping & Relationship Heartbeats

## 1. The Line

**Divorce-related updates are limited to one concise "Status Report" per day; conversely, emotional assurances and "Heartbeat Checks" are unlimited and prioritized.**

## 2. Rationale

To prevent the divorce from becoming the "Operating System" of our relationship. By capping the technical details, I preserve space for us. By increasing assurances, I ensure that my partner feels secure while I am internally preoccupied with external legal and family friction.

## 3. Implementation

### 3.1 The Daily Status Report (DSR)

- **Frequency:** Once per 24-hour cycle.
- **Format:** Factual, concise, and focused on "The What," not "The Feel."
- **The Buffer:** Refer to BND-REL-003 (24-hour rule) for any new "Hard Disclosures."

### 3.2 The Heartbeat Protocol (Assurances)

- **The "Non-Negotiable" Command:** Use explicit, high-certainty language to signal relationship security.
- **Active Check-ins:** During "High-Traffic" events (family gatherings, legal filings), I will initiate 3–5 minute "System Checks" to reassure my partner of her priority.
- **Keyword Phrases:**
    - "You are non-negotiable. Full stop."
    - "I am dealing with [Issue], but my commitment to us is unchanged."

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - The DSR]:** "Here is the 2-minute update on the lawyer meeting today so you're informed. I'm closing that tab now so we can focus on our evening."
- **[SIGNAL - The Heartbeat]:** "I know I'm a bit distracted by my brother's texts right now, but I want you to know: You are my person. You are non-negotiable."

!!! warning "Internal Enforcement"
    Providing assurance is a *gift*, not a *chore*. If I feel resentment while providing assurance, it means I have let the "Divorce CPU Load" get too high and I need to trigger a "System Sleep" (Personal Time) immediately.
