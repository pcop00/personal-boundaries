# Boundary: Information Capping & Relationship Heartbeats

## 1. The Line

**During elevated-stress periods, this protocol can be invoked to prevent any single external situation from consuming the relational space between us — capping updates while keeping assurances high.**

## 2. Rationale

Most of the time, no special structure is needed. When a sustained external situation generates significant noise — a legal filing, a family event, a difficult co-parenting stretch — this protocol protects relational bandwidth without requiring constant renegotiation in the moment. The cap on updates is temporary and situational; the assurances are not.

## 3. Implementation

### 3.1 The Status Report (When Invoked)

- **Trigger:** Invoked during a defined high-friction period, not as a standing daily rule.
- **Format:** One concise factual update per day — "The What," not "The Feel."
- **Buffer:** Any "Hard Disclosures" within the window still follow BND-REL-003 (the 24-hour rule).

### 3.2 The Heartbeat Protocol (Always On)

- **Explicit Assurance:** Use high-certainty language to signal relationship security, especially when preoccupied.
- **Active Check-ins:** During high-stress events, initiate brief "System Checks" to confirm priority.
- **Keyword Phrases:**
    - "You are non-negotiable. Full stop."
    - "I am dealing with [Issue], but my commitment to us is unchanged."

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - Invoking the Cap]:** "This [situation] is generating a lot of noise right now. I'd like to invoke the daily-update cap so it doesn't take over our time together — one update a day, then I'm closing that tab."
- **[SIGNAL - The Heartbeat]:** "I know I'm a bit distracted by [situation] right now, but I want you to know: You are my person. You are non-negotiable."

!!! warning "Internal Check"
    If providing assurance starts to feel like a chore, it is a signal that the external situation has taken up too much space — not that the assurance itself is the problem. Step back, not away.
