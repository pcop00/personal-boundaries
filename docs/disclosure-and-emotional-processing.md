# Boundary: Disclosure & Emotional Processing (Partner)

## 1. The Line

**When I disclose difficult information regarding my divorce or family of origin, there is a mandatory 24-hour "Observation Period" where no advice, critiques, or "fix-it" suggestions are to be offered.**

## 2. Rationale

As I move away from a history of "peace-keeping," I am learning to sit with my own discomfort. When my partner offers an immediate opinion or emotional reaction, my reflexive "fix-it" bias is triggered. This forces me to stop processing my own feelings to manage hers. I need a "sandbox" where I can speak without immediate external feedback.

## 3. Implementation

- **The "Notice of Disclosure":** I will preface sensitive shares with: *"I need to share something, but I am invoking the 24-hour rule."*
- **Active Listening Only:** During the 24-hour window, the goal is "Witnessing," not "Solving."
- **The "Holding Tank":** If my partner has an opinion or an urge to help, she is encouraged to write it down or process it elsewhere, rather than sharing it with me immediately.
- **Consent to Advise:** After 24 hours, the "Write-Lock" is lifted, but advice should still be preceded by: *"Are you ready for some feedback on what you shared yesterday?"*

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - The Intake]:** "I have some hard news about the divorce. I need to tell you so you're in the loop, but I need us to observe the 24-hour rule so I can just sit with it for a bit."
- **[SIGNAL - The Interruption]:** "I know you're trying to help and I love that, but right now my 'fix-it' brain is turning on. Can we put a pin in that advice until tomorrow?"
- **[SIGNAL - The Appreciation]:** "Thank you for just listening and not jumping in. It makes me feel safe to keep sharing these updates with you."

!!! warning "Internal Enforcement"
    If I feel myself trying to "calm her down" regarding a situation that is *happening to me*, I will pause the conversation. It is not my job to manage her emotional reaction to my life's challenges.
