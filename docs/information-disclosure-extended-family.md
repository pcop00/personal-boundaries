# Boundary: Information Disclosure (Extended Family)

## 1. The Line

**I provide factual, logistical updates regarding the divorce process; I do not engage in discussions regarding the moral, theological, or "inner-working" causes of the separation.**

## 2. Rationale

To preserve the health of my familial relationships while navigating a significant life transition. Discussing the "why" or the alignment of my choices with evangelical values creates conflict and emotional exhaustion rather than helpful support.

## 3. Implementation

- **Allow-listed Topics:**
    - Housing and living arrangements.
    - Co-parenting schedules and kids' well-being.
    - Legal timelines and filing milestones.
- **Block-listed Topics:**
    - Theological "covenant" status or reconciliation potential.
    - The "cause" or "who started it" narratives.
    - Current personal values or religious deconstruction.
    - Accusations or speculation (e.g., affairs, "falling away").
- **Active Filtering:** If a conversation shifts from *logistics* to *judgment*, I will apply the [SIGNAL] immediately.

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - The Pivot]:** "I appreciate that you want to be a listening ear, but for our relationship to stay healthy, I'm only sharing the 'what' and 'when' of this process, not the 'why'."
- **[SIGNAL - Theological Deflection]:** "I know your faith is important to you and you're coming from a place of concern, but I am not open to discussing the religious or moral aspects of this decision."
- **[SIGNAL - The "Help" Clarifier]:** "If you want to help, the best thing you can do is [Specific Task, e.g., help with the kids on Tuesday / check in on my move]. I'm not looking for a place to process the details of the marriage itself."

!!! warning "Enforcement Step"
    If family members continue to press for "inner workings" after being redirected, I will end the call or leave the room. I will not justify or defend my life choices to those who are using a value system I no longer subscribe to.
