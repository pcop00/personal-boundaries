# Boundary: Co-Parenting & Personal Autonomy

## 1. The Line

**What exists now is a co-parenting partnership, defined strictly by the children's welfare. I am no longer a provider of proactive personal, logistical, or emotional support.**

## 2. Rationale

The decision to divorce necessitates a redistribution of internal resources. To ensure the stability of the children and my own mental health, I must prioritize self-care and individual autonomy over traditional marital habits of service.

## 3. Implementation

- **Child-First Protocol:** All requests or actions related to the children's health, education, or well-being take absolute priority over all other communications.
- **Request-Only Assistance:** I will not "mind-read" or preemptively solve problems. Assistance is strictly on-demand and subject to my current availability.
- **Digital Boundaries:** Communication should be restricted to child-related logistics. Personal life updates or "venting" will be met with the [SIGNAL] below.
- **Task Separation:** Household, automotive, or administrative tasks for your residence are no longer my responsibility.

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - Emotional Support]:** "I understand this is difficult, but we are no longer each other's emotional support system. I need you to lean on your friends or family for this."
- **[SIGNAL - Proactive Expectation]:** "I wasn't aware you needed help with that. Moving forward, please send a specific request rather than assuming I will step in."
- **[SIGNAL - Priority Check]:** "Right now, I am focusing on the kids' needs. If this isn't about them, I'll have to get back to you later (or not at all)."

!!! warning "Enforcement Step"
    If the boundary between "Co-parent" and "Partner" is blurred, I will pause the conversation and resume it only once the topic returns to the children's welfare.
