# Boundary: Acts of Service — Offered vs. Extracted

## 1. The Line

**Service given freely from a place of love is a gift. Service given because someone asked and I know I will say yes to avoid friction is a transaction that depletes me. I will not conflate the two.**

## 2. Rationale

Acts of service are one of the ways I show love and care — that is not the problem. The problem is when the pattern becomes predictable enough that people begin extracting service rather than receiving it. They ask not because I am better positioned to help, but because they know I will not say no. Over time this conditions a dynamic where my helpfulness is treated as a given, declining feels abnormal, and the meaning of the service is quietly eroded. Resentment follows. The act looked the same from the outside, but the internal experience was entirely different.

## 3. Implementation

- **The "Either of Us Could" Test:** Before acting on a request, I ask: is there a genuine reason this person cannot do this themselves, or are we equally positioned and they are simply asking because they know I will? If the answer is "equally positioned," that is a flag to pause.
- **The Pause:** I do not respond immediately to requests for small acts of service. A brief internal check is not rudeness — it is honesty. The result may still be yes, but it will be a chosen yes.
- **The Authentic Response:** A calm, unapologetic no is as valid as a yes. Neither requires explanation or justification for routine requests.
- **Recalibrating the Dynamic:** Consistently saying yes trains people to always ask. Saying no occasionally — without drama — recalibrates the expectation without confrontation. This is not withholding; it is conditioning a healthier pattern.

## 4. Communication (Signals)

- **[SIGNAL - The Redirect]:** "You're closer than I am — can you grab it?"
- **[SIGNAL - The Honest No]:** "I'm comfortable right now. I'd rather not."
- **[SIGNAL - The Chosen Yes]:** *(only when genuinely freely given)* "Sure, I've got it."

!!! warning "Internal Check"
    If I notice resentment after completing a small act of service, the yes was not freely given. That resentment is data — not a reason to comply more quietly, but a signal to recalibrate. The fix is to say no more often until the dynamic resets.
